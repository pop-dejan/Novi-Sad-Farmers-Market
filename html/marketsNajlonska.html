About Najlonska market

The Najlonska market was created spontaneously in the sixties of the last century on the right side of Temerinski Road. At first, it consisted of two rows of nylons and newspapers placed on the ground, on which citizens brought out and sold worn-out clothes and shoes, old defective household appliances and some other second-hand goods.

By moving to the left side of Temerinski Road, the Najlonska market grew over time into a real "flea" market, with a wide range of various second-hand goods, antiques, used cars, furniture, lumber, firewood, livestock and feed and pets.

Today, Najlonska market covers an area of about 38000 square meters. Out of a total of 2,871 sales points, 509 are intended for the sale of used cars, and there are 25 business premises and 3 refrigerated showcases in the built-up buildings within the market.

On the basis of the new regulatory plan, the Najlonska market was significantly expanded in 2003 and 2004, and extensive works were carried out on the arrangement of the plateau, infrastructure, construction of entrance and exit ramps, two sanitary facilities for tenants and visitors were built... Adaptation is also planned of the surrounding streets to the needs of this market, which primarily refers to a significant increase in the number of parking spaces for visitors, of which there are sometimes more than 50,000 on Sundays.

The development program foresees that in the following years this market will be completely modernized in several stages and become equal to its current models, the famous "flea promenades" in Paris, Rome, London...
