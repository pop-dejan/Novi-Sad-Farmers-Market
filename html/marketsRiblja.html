About Riblja market

With the longest tradition in the city, the Riblja market is a place that, in the middle of the 19th century, served exclusively to supply citizens with fresh fish, evolving over time into a classic green market, rich in live fish, offering fresh products.

On an area of 9000 square meters, this market is equipped with about 500 sales points, 54 business spaces and 50 sales points with refrigerated showcases intended for the sale of milk and milk products.

At the end of 2014, the renovation of the oldest market in the city began. As the plan envisages the renovation of shops and stalls, care is taken to preserve the spirit of the market.

The fish market is located in a very attractive location, between the inner city center and the Danube bank. The management's strategic commitment is to include this market in the tourist offer of the city, which is why various themed events are regularly organized. Cooperation in this field was achieved with relevant institutions, first of all with the Tourism Organization of Novi Sad, which recognized the importance of events such as the Zlatibor market, Days of the Leskovac Grill, Museum Night - Urbanluk, Tastes of Tradition, Novi Sad Stories, Novi Sad Night Bazaar and many others.
